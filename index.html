Answers

Common questions before you reach out.

How can this be free to me?
The landlord pays the brokerage commission — typically 4–6% of the total lease value, split between the listing broker and the tenant's broker (me). If you go direct to the listing agent, the landlord pays the same amount but you get no representation. There's no scenario where using a tenant rep costs you money.
Why not just call the phone number on the sign?
You can. But the person on that sign represents the landlord. Their job is to get the highest rent with the fewest concessions. When you negotiate alone, you don't know what other buildings are asking, what the landlord's actual floor is, or what's on the verge of expiring and about to come back on market.
How do I know Zach is credible?
Florida license #SL3603483 — verifiable on the Florida DBPR website. Brokerage is Cornerstone Realty. 50,000+ SF of warehouse space leased for tenants in Palm Beach County. I'll share client references for any specific submarket on request.
What information do you need from me?
The form above — type, size, timeline, and contact info — is enough to send a first round of matches. If you want sharper matches, include budget, industry, and building specs (clear height, dock doors, power). No credit check, no commitment.
Can you help with sales, not just leases?
Yes. Industrial condos and owner-user buildings for sale are part of my practice. Tell me on the form (or when we talk) that you're considering buying vs. leasing and I'll run the math both ways.
What if I'm just exploring and don't need space for 6+ months?
Even better — earlier conversations mean better matches. I'll send you market updates monthly for the submarket you're watching, with no pressure. When you're ready, you'll already know what a fair deal looks like.
