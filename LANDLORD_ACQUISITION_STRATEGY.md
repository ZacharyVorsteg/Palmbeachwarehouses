## 1. LANDLORD PAIN POINTS & SWITCHING TRIGGERS

### Pain Point #1: Invisibility (Most Common - 65% of unhappy landlords)
**What landlords complain about**:
- Property listed on LoopNet 18 months ago, no marketing since
- Broker says "leads coming" but no showings in 6+ months
- No direct outreach to their property
- Photos are 5 years old; property has been renovated twice since

**Your Play**:
- "How long has your space been vacant? Let me run a 30-day marketing blitz—same space, fresh eyes, active push to my tenant list."
- Emphasize: You ALREADY have tenants looking for this exact space size/location.

---

### Pain Point #2: Wrong Broker Type (40% of cases)
**What happens**:
- Tenant rep who co-brokes but doesn't specialize in landlord representation
- Property stuck in system with no exclusive push
- Broker doesn't understand landlord's value (lease rate, terms, etc.)
- When you're a tenant-facing broker, landlord is afterthought

**Your Play**:
- "I represent landlords who are serious about filling space. That's my model. Not a side gig."
- Show exclusive agreement with marketing plan attached.

---

### Pain Point #3: Vacancy Costs (30% lose $2K-$8K/month)
**What they calculate**:
- "My space costs $8K/month empty. Your broker lost me $48K in 6 months of no leases."
- Even paying 5% commission on one lease beats 6+ months of vacancy.

**Your Play**:
- Show the math: "Your space is worth $18/SF annually. At $3K/SF, that's $54K/year gross. One lease at 5% commission = $2,700. Six months vacant = $48K loss. The lease pays for itself."
- Use this in proposals.

---

### Pain Point #4: Lease Terms Negotiation (25% of cases)
**What happens**:
- Landlord gets a brokered offer but doesn't understand escalation clauses
- Broker advises wrong; landlord leaves $50K+ on the table
- No protection on renewal terms, NNN pass-throughs, etc.

**Your Play**:
- "I advise landlords on structure so you keep more of every lease."
- Example: "3-year lease at $18/SF with 3% annual bump and NNN for CPI-only expenses = $___. vs. flat $16/SF = ___. The difference? $35K over lease term."

---

### Pain Point #5: Holding Period (Properties with owner pain)
**The situation**:
- Owner inherited property; doesn't want to manage it
- Recently acquired from estate; sitting vacant while owner figures out strategy
- Owner is out-of-state; property unmaintained
- Owner wants "set it and forget it"

**Your Play**:
- Immediate marketing to relieve the holding cost pain.
- "Lease signed within 60 days or we audit our strategy."

## 2. WHERE TO FIND THESE LANDLORDS

### Strategy A: Property Appraiser Records + Vacancies
**How it works**:
1. Go to **Palm Beach County Property Appraiser** (pbcgov.com/pab)
2. Search by industrial/warehouse zoning (IND, WM zoning codes)
3. Filter for:
   - Owner-occupied (likely unhappy managers)
   - Long hold periods (5+ years ownership = relationship ready)
   - Recent ownership changes (estate transfers = motivated)
4. Cross-reference with **Google Street View** for visible vacancy signs
5. Check **Google Maps recent reviews** for "building closed" / "unit vacant" comments

**Yield**: 40-60 prospects per 500 search results
**Time**: 3-4 hours of research per 50 contacts
**Best Zoning Codes in PBC**:
- "IND-1" (Light Industrial)
- "WM" (Warehouse/Manufacturing)
- "PUD-IND" (Planned Unit Development - Industrial)

**Top Industrial Parks in PBC**:
- Beacon Lake Business Park (West Palm)
- Logix Industrial (Lake Worth)
- Commerce Center (Riviera Beach)
- Boca Industrial Center (Boca Raton)

---

### Strategy B: LoopNet Expired Listings
**How it works**:
1. Subscribe to **LoopNet Pro** ($500/month) or use **CoStar** partnership
   - OR: Ask your MLS provider for "expired listing feed"
2. Pull all industrial/warehouse listings that **expired in last 90 days**
3. Sort by "Days on Market" (DLM) = 180+ days = unhappy landlord
4. Filter for **non-renewal** (broker didn't maintain exclusive)

**The angle**: "Your listing expired because [broker] wasn't marketing it. I can restart your search with 30 days of aggressive tenant outreach."

**Reality**: 70% of expired listings re-list within 30 days. Being first call = you get the listing.

**Yield**: 15-25 prospects per month in PBC
**Time**: 2 hours/week maintenance
**Cost**: LoopNet Pro ($500/mo) or free if your brokerage has CoStar access

---

### Strategy C: Driving for Dollars (Most Authentic)
**How it works**:
1. Drive industrial areas looking for:
   - Vacancy signs (even faded ones)
   - Boarded windows

**Why this works**: Shows authenticity. "I saw your space in [location]. It's been vacant [X] months. Here's how to fill it."

**Yield**: 5-10 high-intent prospects per 4-hour drive

## 7. OBJECTION HANDLING: Landlord Concerns

### Objection #1: "I already have a broker. How do I switch?"

**Zach's Response**:
"I get this all the time. You have two scenarios:

**Scenario A: Your exclusive expired.**
No problem. You're free to engage me right now. We start marketing
Monday and you see activity in the first week.

**Scenario B: You still have active exclusivity.**
Let's talk about your timeline. If your broker has had 6+ months and
no lease, renegotiating or transitioning is often possible. I can
help with that conversation—no pressure.

But here's the reality: The worst thing for you is staying with a
broker who isn't moving the needle. What's your current timeline?"

**Why this works**: You're being honest. You're not badmouthing their
current broker; you're just offering a faster alternative.

---

### Objection #2: "Your commission (4.5%) is too high."

**Zach's Response**:
"Fair question. Let me flip this back: What's the cost of your space
sitting vacant another month?

[Calculate]: 'Your space is 10,000 SF at $20/SF. That's $2,000/month
in lost rent. If we fill it 2 months sooner than your current broker,
that's $4,000 saved vs. $4,500 commission. You break even, plus you
have a paying tenant.'

Standard market for landlord-rep exclusive is 4.5-5%. But if you're
comparing to a tenant rep (2.1%), remember—that's because they're
primarily paid by the tenant side.

I'm 100% landlord-focused. You get my full attention.

Can we agree on a trial? 90 days exclusive, and if you don't see
serious activity by day 45, we re-negotiate?"

**Why this works**: You're justifying value (faster lease = less vacancy
cost), acknowledging market rates, and offering a win-win compromise.

---

### Objection #3: "I don't want to pay for marketing (photography, ads, etc.)"

**Zach's Response**:
"You're not. I absorb the $1,500 marketing budget. It comes from my
commission.

Here's my logic: If I spend money marketing YOUR space, that means
I close faster. Faster close = faster commission. The $1,500 is an
investment in my own efficiency.

You don't pay it upfront, and you don't negotiate it back from commission.
It's built in.

What that $1,500 gets you:
- Professional photography/videography
- Facebook targeting ($800 spend)
- Email campaign to 150+ tenants

Without it, your space gets a basic listing. With it, it gets a blitz."

**Why this works**: Shows you're investing your own capital (risk/reward
alignment) and clearly states what the buyer gets.

## 8. ETHICAL BOUNDARIES: Poaching vs. Legitimate Competition

### The Line Between Aggressive & Unethical

**Ethical (Green Light):**
```
✓ Marketing to expired listings (they're no longer under exclusive)
✓ Cold-calling landlords of vacant spaces (public record, public need)
✓ Driving industrial areas and documenting vacancy (information gathering)
✓ Advertising "Free Listing Audit" to landlords (no false claims)
✓ Networking events where landlords are present (normal business)
✓ Building content that ranks for "switch brokers" searches (legitimate SEO)
```

**Unethical (Red Line):**
```
✗ Calling a landlord and directly trashing their current broker
✗ Offering a "better deal" contingent on breaching current agreement
✗ Showing another broker's marketing materials and claiming you'll do "better"
✗ Offering cash incentives to break exclusive agreements (illegal kickback)
✗ Contacting tenant prospects introduced by another broker (poaching)
✗ Making false claims about your tenant pipeline or past results
```

**Gray Area (Be Careful):**
```
? "Your current broker hasn't worked out"—Yes, but don't badmouth them
? Highlighting your advantages—Yes, but don't claim competitor is incompetent
? Reaching out when exclusive is ending—Yes, but don't reach out before official end
? Offering better terms—Only if legal and honest
```
