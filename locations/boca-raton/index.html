Boca Raton Industrial Market Overview

$16-24 /SF NNN Annual
30-50% Premium vs. WPB
Class A Dominant Building Type

Boca Raton has some of the most sought-after industrial and flex space in Palm Beach County. The market skews toward technology companies, e-commerce, and professional services - businesses that need quality space and don't mind paying for it.

Expect to pay $16-24/SF NNN in Boca Raton, sometimes higher for premium Class A flex space in parks like Broken Sound or Park of Commerce. That's 30-50% more than West Palm Beach, but you get newer buildings, better office finishes, and a different tenant profile.

Boca Raton Industrial Areas

  • Broken Sound / Park of Commerce - Class A flex and office-warehouse, highest rents in the county ($20-28/SF)
  • Arvida Park of Commerce - Corporate campuses, flex space, tech tenants
  • Congress Avenue Corridor - Mix of flex and traditional industrial, moderate pricing ($16-20/SF)
  • Yamato Road / I-95 - Tech-focused parks, modern infrastructure, easy highway access
  • Glades Road Area - Distribution and logistics, good I-95 connectivity
  • Clint Moore Road - Newer flex developments, growing submarket

Why Tenants Pay the Boca Premium

Boca Raton commands higher rents due to: Class A building quality, proximity to affluent residential areas for employee recruitment, excellent I-95 and Florida's Turnpike access, and limited new industrial land availability. Many technology, life sciences, and corporate headquarters prefer Boca for image and workforce access.

Is Boca Right for You?

If image matters to your business and you need modern space with quality finishes, Boca Raton is worth the premium. If you're more focused on getting the most square footage for your dollar, I'd steer you toward West Palm Beach where you can save 30-50% on rent. Either way, I'll give you honest advice about what makes sense for your situation.
