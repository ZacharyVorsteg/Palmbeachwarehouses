Lantana Industrial Market Overview

$14-20 /SF NNN Annual
Concentrated Industrial Market
Direct I-95 Easy Accessibility

Lantana is a compact industrial market offering focused opportunity for landlords and tenants alike. The concentrated nature of the submarket means specialized market knowledge, stable landlord portfolios, and a defined set of property types. Businesses benefit from less shopping time, faster leasing decisions, and better familiarity with landlord operations.

The North Broadway corridor is Lantana's industrial spine, hosting a mix of warehousing, light manufacturing, and distribution facilities. This concentration creates secondary tenancy opportunities: a successful operation in one building attracts compatible neighbors and shared-service providers.

Expect to pay $14-20/SF NNN in Lantana — the most competitive rates in Southern Palm Beach County. That's below neighboring Lake Worth ($16-24/SF) and Boynton Beach ($16-22/SF), making Lantana ideal for startup distributors, emerging 3PL operators, and value-conscious tenants.

Lantana Industrial Areas

  • North Broadway Industrial Corridor - Lantana's primary industrial spine with warehousing, light manufacturing, and distribution in concentrated clusters. 70% of industrial activity ($16-20/SF)
  • South Federal Highway Value Zone - Smaller, divisible spaces at aggressive pricing. Ideal for emerging operators and small-business warehousing ($14-18/SF)
  • Lantana Road Mixed-Use Zone - Flex warehouse and mixed-use facilities blending industrial and retail. Showroom-plus-warehouse operations ($14-17/SF)

Why Lantana Delivers Value

Lantana's direct I-95 access with immediate on/off-ramps creates unmatched regional connectivity. Miami metro is 30 minutes south, Fort Lauderdale 30 minutes north, and Orlando 3.5 hours west. This positioning makes Lantana ideal for regional distributors managing tight delivery windows and e-commerce fulfillment centers.

Is Lantana Right for You?

If you're looking for the most square footage for your dollar in Southern Palm Beach County, Lantana is hard to beat. Small contractors, specialty trades, and e-commerce fulfillment operations thrive here — the rents leave room in the budget for equipment and inventory investment. If you need larger Class A facilities or a premium business address, I'd point you toward Boca Raton or Boynton Beach instead.

Local Market Insider: Lantana

Lantana occupies a sweet spot between the higher-priced Boynton Beach and Lake Worth markets. The Hypoluxo Road and Lantana Road corridors provide easy I-95 access, and the area's smaller industrial parks keep overhead manageable for growing businesses. You're close enough to serve coastal clients in Manalapan and Hypoluxo but paying interior rents.

Tenants pick Lantana for value without sacrifice. The commute to central Palm Beach County is straightforward via I-95 or Dixie Highway, and you're positioned to serve both southern and central PBC customers. Small contractors, specialty trades, and e-commerce fulfillment operations thrive here — the rents leave room in the budget for equipment and inventory investment instead of overhead.
