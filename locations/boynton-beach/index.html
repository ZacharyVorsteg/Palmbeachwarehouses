Boynton Beach Industrial Market Overview

$16-22 /SF NNN Annual
Growing Market Trend
Congress Ave Main Corridor

Boynton Beach is where Palm Beach County's industrial market is expanding. Congress Avenue runs north-south through the center of the market, anchoring clusters of modern flex parks, Class A logistics facilities, and growing inventory. The city has attracted serious industrial development in recent years, making it a sweet spot between the value of Lake Worth and the premium of Boca Raton.

Expect to pay $16-22/SF NNN in Boynton Beach. Strong market growth has made it a competitive option between Lake Worth and Boca Raton, with newer buildings, modern amenities, and active leasing activity driving tenant demand.

Boynton Beach Industrial Areas

  • Congress Avenue Corridor (N-S Spine) - The primary industrial district. Boynton's heaviest concentration of flex parks, class A warehouses, and modern logistics facilities. This is where most new development and leasing activity happens.
  • Quantum Park - Dos Lagos - The high-visibility submarket. Home to the highest concentration of active industrial listings. Modern, mixed-use industrial park with strong tenant visibility and Class A amenities.
  • Lantana Road Area - Secondary industrial zone with good I-95 access and solid logistics operators. More affordable than Congress Ave but still well-maintained.
  • Boynton Lakes Submarket - Emerging area with newer buildings and improving infrastructure. Good for tenants seeking modern space without Congress Ave visibility prices.
  • Hypoluxo Road / Federal Highway - East-side industrial with good frontage. Mixed retail-industrial corridor, popular with smaller users and distribution.

Why Boynton Beach Is Attracting Tenants

Boynton Beach sits at the crossroads of central Palm Beach County's industrial expansion. Congress Avenue gives you visibility and access. Quantum Park - Dos Lagos offers institutional-quality facilities. Highway access via I-95 and the Florida Turnpike is excellent. Strong market fundamentals and active leasing are drawing tenants seeking modern space with excellent connectivity.

The Boynton Tenant Profile

Boynton Beach attracts growing businesses, established 3PLs, flex tenants, light manufacturing, and companies that need visibility on a major corridor. It's the market for tenants seeking modern industrial space with excellent highway connectivity and strong visibility.

Local Market Insider: Boynton Beach

Boynton Beach benefits from its central PBC positioning — equidistant from West Palm Beach and Boca Raton via I-95, with Congress Avenue providing a high-traffic commercial corridor. Quantum Park, originally planned as a tech campus, has evolved into a hybrid industrial-office cluster that attracts light manufacturing, lab space, and tech-adjacent businesses. The Congress Ave growth between Gateway and Woolbright has added newer retail and commercial development, pulling service businesses into the area.

Tenants choose Boynton Beach for the balance: better rents than Boca, more modern inventory than Lake Worth, and a central location that minimizes drive time to anywhere in the county. If you serve customers across all of PBC, Boynton is the geographic center of gravity — no client is more than 30 minutes away.
